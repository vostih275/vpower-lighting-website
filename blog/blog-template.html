Behind the Craft

Stories from the workshop where tradition meets innovation

The Journey of a Barn Beam: From Weathered Wood to Timeless Light

By James Mitchell • November 15, 2024

Last month, we acquired a remarkable 150-year-old barn beam from a family farm in Lancaster County. As I ran my hands over the weathered surface, I could feel the history embedded in every grain and knot. This wasn't just wood—it was a timeline of seasons, storms, and the daily rhythms of farm life.

The transformation process began with careful deconstruction. We spent three days simply studying the beam, understanding its character, and planning how best to honor its journey. The challenge was preserving the authentic weathered surface while ensuring structural integrity for its new life as a chandelier.

What emerged was "The Heritage Beam"—a six-light chandelier that maintains the original patina while incorporating modern LED technology. Each light socket is hand-forged and positioned to highlight the natural beauty of the wood. When installed in our client's farmhouse dining room, it created the perfect bridge between past and present.

This is what we love about our work—every piece tells a story, and we're just the latest chapter in its journey.

The Art of Metalworking: Forging Iron with Fire and Tradition

By Sarah Chen • October 28, 2024

There's something primal about working with iron and fire. As the lead metalworker at VPOWER, I spend my days surrounded by the rhythmic sounds of hammer striking steel and the glow of our coal forge. Each piece we create begins as raw iron stock, unremarkable in its initial state, but full of potential.

Today, I'm working on a custom pendant light for a mountain lodge in Colorado. The client wants something that feels both rugged and refined—a challenge that requires balancing raw texture with precise craftsmanship. I start by heating the iron to 2,200 degrees Fahrenheit until it glows a bright orange, then begin the dance of hammer and anvil.

The process is meditative. Each strike of the hammer must be deliberate—too hard and you risk cracking the metal, too soft and you won't achieve the desired form. I'm creating twisted elements that will wrap around the light socket, reminiscent of grapevines you might find climbing the walls of old mountain cabins.

After hours of work, the piece emerges—still hot from the forge, bearing the marks of its creation. These aren't imperfections; they're the signature of traditional craftsmanship, the evidence that human hands, not machines, brought this piece to life.

Sustainable Lighting: Why Reclaimed Materials Matter

By James Mitchell • September 12, 2024

In today's world, sustainability isn't just a buzzword—it's a responsibility. At VPOWER, we've built our entire business around the principle of giving new life to materials that would otherwise be discarded. But what does that really mean in practice?

Every month, we divert approximately 2,000 pounds of wood and metal from landfills. Our reclaimed wood comes from deconstructed barns, old factories, and even fallen trees from local properties. Each piece is carefully assessed for structural integrity and character before entering our workshop.

The environmental impact extends beyond just material reuse. Our finishing process uses zero-VOC oils and waxes, and we've designed our lighting to work efficiently with LED technology, reducing energy consumption for our customers. We even power part of our workshop using solar panels installed on our roof.

But sustainability isn't just about environmental impact—it's about preserving stories. When you choose a VPOWER fixture, you're not just getting a light; you're becoming part of a material's ongoing story. That barn beam that once sheltered livestock now illuminates family dinners. The factory steel that supported machinery now casts warm light in a cozy reading nook.

This is the future we believe in—one where beauty, functionality, and sustainability coexist in perfect harmony.
